# WICYS-Cross-Chapter-CTF-2026
A collection of the challenges I designed for the WICYS cross chapter CTF of 2026. The CTF was run on Linode servers using CTFd.

---

### Theme
One week before Easter, the Bunny Network boots up its final prep run—basket routing, egg schedules, candy inventory, and secure comms. At exactly 00:00, everything drops for seven seconds. When it returns, routes loop into nowhere, counts turn negative, and a single scrambled distress ping from the Easter Bunny’s tracker flashes from a location that doesn’t exist on any map… then vanishes.
A hijacked broadcast cuts into the control channel: Heap MacCipher, a leprechaun who treats “luck” like an exploit chain. He isn’t stealing chocolate—he’s stealing probability. If he can make Easter fail before it even begins, he can prove the world runs on luck—and sell “luck insurance” back to everyone at a premium. To stall you, he’s planted traps across every part of the operation: encrypted comms, compromised portals, staged evidence, corrupted files, and “holiday updates” that are anything but festive.
You’re the Spring Incident Response Team, called in for a two-day emergency response while there’s still a chance to salvage Easter prep. Every challenge you solve restores a piece of the pipeline—recovering keys, validating what’s real, undoing sabotage, and following the Bunny’s last true signal through layers of misdirection.
As you peel back the decoys, the clues converge on one place: the Rainbow Vault—Heap MacCipher’s hidden stash site where he’s holding the Easter Bunny and powering the scheme that’s choking the prep systems. Find the way in, shut down the luck engine, and extract the Bunny as soon as possible—because every hour he’s gone is an hour Easter can’t be rebuilt.
